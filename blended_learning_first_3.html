How prestigious universities started spreading education online

During the past ten years, especially since 2012, prestigious universities began converting free online lectures that anyone could view, they converted them through educational platforms such as edX and Coursera into courses taught online with the same quality of education within these universities, and for those who study these courses , they can obtain a verified certificate from these prestigious universities, or even credit hours that can be accredited.

This is a great leap in education because as a student, if I watch lectures in a subject in physics, for example, from MIT, how can I prove to the university in which I am that I understand this subject? Offering accredited and semi-free courses from major universities via the Internet opened the way for any student who wants to obtain the best education at the lowest cost, and this will only be done with the encouragement and support of the student by the local universities in the country in which this student seeks blended learning with the best universities In the world. Making regulations through local universities that allow gifted students the possibility of blended learning with the best universities in the world is considered a guarantee of the student’s right so that he will not be required to study these subjects again within the local university, so that the student gets the appreciation and evaluation that he deserves and these subjects which he studied online get included within a bachelor's degree.

Definition of “Massive Open Online Courses” or “MOOCs”:

Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) are free online courses available to anyone to enroll. MOOCs provide an affordable and flexible way to learn new skills, advance your career, and deliver high-quality learning experiences at scale.
